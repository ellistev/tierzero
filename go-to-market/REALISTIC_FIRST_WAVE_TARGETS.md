# TierZero Realistic First-Wave Targets

## Purpose
This is the corrected first-wave target list for TierZero.

It is optimized for **reachable design-partner buyers**, not prestige logos.
The filter is simple:
- obvious queue pain
- lean enough team to care
- small enough buying surface to move
- believable first pilot scope
- enough operational maturity to run a narrow workflow safely

## Ranking logic
### Priority A
Best first outreach.
Either they are already selling or running help desk operations all day, or they are a lower-friction operator with direct evidence of ITSM / ServiceNow footprint.

### Priority B
Good second wave.
Still realistic, but slightly more procurement drag, weaker speed-to-yes, or a less direct pain signal.

## Best-fit buyer titles
- Owner / Founder
- VP Managed Services
- Director of Service Delivery
- Help Desk Manager
- Head of IT
- Director of IT Operations
- ServiceNow Administrator / Platform Owner
- CIO at sub-enterprise scale

## Priority A - go here first

### 1. Mission Control
- **Type:** outsourced help desk / MSP support partner
- **Why this is realistic:** They literally sell outsourced help desk and NOC support to MSPs. That means they live inside ticket volume, after-hours coverage, and overflow pain already. This is exactly the kind of buyer who can understand and trial narrow queue automation fast.
- **Procurement reality:** low to medium. Likely far faster than enterprise internal IT.
- **Source note:** Mission Control says it is the outsourced help desk for MSPs, provides white-label 24x7x365 support, and serves MSPs, IT support companies, and vendors.
- **Source:** https://www.missioncontrolnoc.com/
- **Likely buyers:** Owner, VP Managed Services, Director of Service Delivery, Help Desk Manager
- **Priority:** A1

### 2. IT By Design
- **Type:** MSP help desk partner
- **Why this is realistic:** Their helpdesk page explicitly says they act as an extension of the MSP team, work in PSA systems like ConnectWise and Autotask, and reduce overloaded internal queues with 24/7 support. That is basically screaming for one repetitive-ticket automation wedge.
- **Procurement reality:** low to medium.
- **Source note:** IT By Design says its technicians work in the MSP's system, deliver 24/7 white-labeled support, and provide scalable staffing to reduce burnout and churn.
- **Source:** https://itbd.net/services/improved-it-helpdesk-support/
- **Likely buyers:** VP Managed Services, Service Delivery Director, Help Desk Manager, COO
- **Priority:** A1

### 3. Infrassist
- **Type:** managed help desk for MSPs
- **Why this is realistic:** Their positioning is pure support-pain language: resource shortages, relentless support demands, overloaded queues, and 24/7 coverage gaps. Good fit for a design-partner pitch around taking one repetitive ticket class off the board.
- **Procurement reality:** low to medium.
- **Source note:** Infrassist says service desk is at the core of every MSP's operations, offers 24/7 managed help desk coverage, and works in the MSP's PSA/ticketing system.
- **Source:** https://www.infrassist.com/managed-help-desk-services/
- **Likely buyers:** Owner, Service Delivery Director, Help Desk Manager, VP Operations
- **Priority:** A1

### 4. Worksent

## Who got cut on purpose
These were deprioritized because they are more likely to waste time than buy fast:
- giant global manufacturers
- major hospital systems with heavy procurement drag
- Fortune 500 prestige logos
- giant banks and insurers without a warm path

## Best immediate first five
If the goal is speed, start here:
1. Mission Control
2. IT By Design
3. Infrassist
4. Worksent
5. Ovation Healthcare

## Why these five first
- the pain is explicit
- the buyer surface is smaller
- the pitch is easy to understand
- the pilot can be framed as a design-partner engagement instead of enterprise transformation theater
