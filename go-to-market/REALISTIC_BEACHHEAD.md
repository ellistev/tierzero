# TierZero Realistic Beachhead

## Reality check
TierZero is not going to win its first deal by cold-pitching giant enterprises that can bury a solo founder in procurement, security review, and credibility questions.

The first sale needs to be:
- reachable
- fast-moving
- painful enough to care
- small enough to say yes
- operationally messy enough to value relief

## Correct first-customer profile
### Best first buyers
1. **Regional MSPs / outsourced help desks**
   - 10-100 support agents
   - already live inside ticket queues all day
   - feel backlog pain directly
   - can pilot on one customer-facing or internal workflow fast

2. **Multi-site SMB or lower-mid-market operators**
   - roughly 100-1000 employees
   - one lean IT team supporting many locations
   - examples: regional healthcare groups, logistics operators, manufacturers, retail groups, franchise operators

3. **IT leaders with direct buying power**
   - not a committee of 12 strangers
   - ideally one Head of IT, IT Ops lead, or owner-operator who can say yes to a small pilot

## Bad first buyers
Avoid for now:
- giant enterprise logos used for ego fuel
- national hospital systems with heavy procurement drag
- major banks and insurers with endless risk review
- Fortune 500 companies unless there is a warm path and a specific champion
- any buyer where case-study risk, compliance burden, and sales-cycle length are bigger than the pilot itself

## Better first wedge positioning
Do not sell "autonomous tier-0 for the enterprise".
Sell:
**"Take one repetitive ticket class off a lean support team's plate without losing control."**

That is believable.
That can get bought.

## Better first workflow
Keep the workflow narrow:
- password reset / account unlock
- basic access-request triage
- one runbook-backed fulfillment request

But the real promise is not the workflow name.
It is:
- smaller queue
- fewer low-value touches
- cleaner handoff on exceptions

## Better commercial motion
### First 2-3 customers
Treat them as **design partners**, not full enterprise accounts.

Suggested shape:
- 2-4 week setup
- 14-30 day pilot
- lower-friction price point than the current enterprise-style pilot math
- explicit feedback loop
- case study or testimonial upside if it works

### Suggested early pricing posture
More realistic first-design-partner range:
- setup: **$3k-$10k**
- monthly pilot: **$2k-$6k**

Raise pricing after proof.
Do not cosplay as a mature enterprise vendor before the product earns it.

## What to optimize for right now
1. A buyer who feels the pain personally
2. A yes/no decision in weeks, not quarters
3. One workflow with obvious before/after value
4. Fast feedback from a real environment
5. A credible proof story you can reuse

## New qualification rules
A target is good only if most of this is true:
- reachable decision-maker
- lean support team
- obvious repetitive ticket pain
- low enough procurement drag to close a pilot
- enough trust to try one narrow workflow
- clear expansion path if the first workflow works

## New anti-goals
- Do not optimize for logo prestige
- Do not build the first outbound around companies that need a vendor packet, legal review, security review, and six internal champions before a tiny pilot
- Do not confuse "big company" with "good early customer"

## Immediate strategy shift
The next target slate should bias toward:
- regional MSPs
- sub-enterprise multi-site operators
- lower-mid-market healthcare/logistics/manufacturing/retail orgs
- warm-network or second-degree reachable buyers where possible

The current giant-account list is not useless, but it should not drive the first outbound wave.
